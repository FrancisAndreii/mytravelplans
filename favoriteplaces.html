Intramuros

  • 1. Intramuros

    • Intramuros is the historic walled city of Manila dating back to the Spanish colonial period. This area provides a glimpse into the rich history and culture of the Philippines with its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture, slower pace of life, and old-world charm that differs from the hectic Manila metropolis. Taking a leisurely kalesa (horse-drawn carriage) ride around the cobblestone streets enhances the nostalgic, peaceful experience. Charming accommodations like colonial-style hotels and inns are available. There are also local restaurants serving comforting Filipino cuisine like adobo and sinigang. Entertainers perform at various plazas, providing laid-back entertainment. For visitors seeking tranquility, history, and comfort, Intramuros offers a secure retreat. Its nostalgic atmosphere harkens back to historic Old Manila. You can admire the Spanish-era forts, churches, and landmarks while imagining what life was like centuries ago. The familiar cuisine and slow-paced lifestyle contrasts the bustling metro. Overall, the walled city's old-world ambiance makes it an ideal place to unwind and understand the Philippines' past.

    Sea Side, Mall of Asia

  • 2. Sea Side, Mall of Asia

    • SM Mall of Asia is a massive shopping and entertainment complex located along Manila Bay in Pasay City. With over 800 shops, restaurants, and attractions, it spans 42 hectares and provides beautiful seaside views. Some highlights include the large open central square hosting concerts and events, a covered outdoor esplanade, and a 430-meter long seaside promenade. You can go ice skating at the Olympic-sized ice rink, or relax by the fountain and viewpoint gazing at the Manila Bay sunset. For shopping, there is a 3-level main mall with international brands, a department store, and supermarket. The south wing houses local Filipino shops and bazaars selling handicrafts, pasalubong, and more. There are also four themed shopping districts - Fashion Avenue, Gadget Zone, Northwing, and Cyberzone. Dining options abound from upscale restaurants to food courts offering a wide array of Filipino and international cuisines. Movie theaters and an IMAX cinema provide entertainment as well. Nearby are the SMX Convention Center, Arena, and MOA Eye Ferris Wheel. With its seaside location, variety of stores, dining and entertainment in one massive complex, Mall of Asia attracts millions of local and foreign visitors each year. It's a convenient one-stop shop for shopping, eating, and having fun by the bay.

    Cloud 9, Antipolo

  • 3. Cloud 9, Antipolo

    • Cloud 9 is a popular resort, event space, and tourism destination in Antipolo, Rizal overlooking Metro Manila. Perched at an elevation of 1,000 feet above sea level, it provides panoramic views of the metropolis below. The main attraction is the infinity pool that gives the illusion of extending towards the skyline. Lounging in the pool or poolside on the sunbeds is the perfect way to relax while enjoying the breathtaking scenery. There are also rooms and cottages available for overnight stays. The resort has function halls for events like weddings and parties. The Tivoli Restaurant offers al fresco dining with a view. You can also have a drink at the Cloud 9 Sunset Bar while watching the sunset over the city. Other activities include riding the 230m Cloud 9 Antipolo Zipline, trekking the nature trails, playing basketball, billiards, chess and more. Massages are also available at the spa. Visitors can reach Cloud 9 via private car, taxi, or arranged transportation like a van rental. It's a popular day trip spot from Manila for those looking to escape the city heat while enjoying Antipolo's cooler mountain breeze. With sweeping metropolitan vistas and amenities, Cloud 9 offers an uplifting experience.
